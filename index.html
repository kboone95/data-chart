Make It Stop: Stop-and-Frisk

By: Kayla Boone | November 13, 2020

Stop-and frisk allows police officers to stop, interrogate and search New York City citizens on the sole basis of “reasonable suspicion.” In 2011, under the Bloomberg administration at the height of stop-and-frisk over 685,000 people were stopped. Judge Shira A. Scheindlin ruled that NYPD’s stop-and-frisk tactics violate the U.S Constitution’s 4th Amendment prohibition of unreasonable searches and seizures.
